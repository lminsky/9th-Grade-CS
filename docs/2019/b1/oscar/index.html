Lets start with baby carrots and baby cut carrots

First, what exactly is a baby carrot?

There are two ways people talk about baby carrots:

-- First, there's normal baby carrots which are carrots that haven't reached full maturity yet

-- There's also baby cut carrots which are carrots cut down from fully sized carrots

*These are the type of carrots you will usually think of when talking about baby carrots because they are marketed as "baby carrots"

-- Regular baby carrots are immature carrrots that are harvested, usuallt due to crop thinning. Crop thinning is the process of removing crops to make room for others. Now, some cultivars are specifically breeding carrots to be used at this premature stage. This is because baby carrots tend to be sweeter and more tender than fully mature ones.

-- Baby cut carrots are fully grown carrots that are cut down to a smaller size. This type of carrot was created so that farmers didn't have to waste a whole carrot if there were a few blemishes or rottings on the outside of the carrot, they could simply cut it down.
